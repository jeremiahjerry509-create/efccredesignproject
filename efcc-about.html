Our History Through Time

2002 - 2003

Establishment of EFCC

  • Established by an Act of the National Assembly on December 12, 2002
  • Operational activities commenced on April 13, 2003 under Mallam Nuhu Ribadu
  • Response to pressure from Financial Action Task Force (FATF) on Money Laundering
2004

Legal Framework Update

The 2002 Establishment Act was repealed and re-enacted as the 2004 Establishment Act, signed into law on June 4, 2004 by President Obasanjo, strengthening the Commission's mandate and operations.

2003-2007

Pioneer Leadership

Under Mallam Nuhu Ribadu, EFCC made significant strides in its early years:

  • Successfully combated 419 fraud (advance fee fraud)
  • Established zonal commands across major cities: Lagos, Port Harcourt, Enugu, Kano, and Gombe
  • Prosecuted high-profile cases including Fred Ajudua, Emmanuel Nwude, and other notorious fraudsters
2007-2011

Leadership Transitions

The Commission experienced key leadership changes during this period:

  • Late Mr. Ibrahim Lamorde (Acting, Dec 2007 - June 2008)
  • Farida Waziri (June 2008 - Nov 2011)
2011-2015

Consolidation Phase

Under Ibrahim Lamorde's leadership (Nov 2011 - Nov 2015):

  • Confirmed as substantive Chairman in February 2012
  • Strengthened anti-corruption efforts and international collaborations
  • Enhanced operational efficiency and case management systems
2015-2020

Expansion Era

Ibrahim Magu's leadership (Nov 2015 - July 2020) saw significant expansion:

  • Established seven new commands: Ibadan, Kaduna, Benin, Makurdi, Sokoto, Ilorin, and Uyo
  • Reopened Maiduguri office to strengthen presence in the North East
  • Intensified the fight against corruption with high-profile prosecutions
  • Enhanced international cooperation in anti-corruption efforts
2020-Present

Recent Leadership

Recent leadership transitions and developments:

  • Olanipekun Olukoyede (October 2023 - Present)
  • Abdulkarim Chukkol (Acting, June 2023 - October 2023)
  • Abdulrasheed Bawa (March 2021 - June 2023)
  • Mohammed Umar Abba (Acting, July 2020 - March 2021)

The EFCC continues to evolve and adapt to new challenges in the fight against economic and financial crimes in Nigeria.
